The Shipyard

Why Wauquiez
is different.

Henri Wauquiez founded his shipyard in 1965 in northern France, converting his family's tannery into one of the most respected boatbuilding operations in Europe. From the start, his obsession was quality at the standard of the finest English shipyards.

Hand-built, not mass-produced

Balsa-sandwich decks, carefully selected hardware, interior joinery finished to a level that rivals bespoke furniture. The Amphitrite 45 MS is a serious bluewater cruiser — centre cockpit, elevated deck saloon, lead keel. Designed to cross oceans.

Designed by Holman & Pye

The British naval architecture firm behind many of Wauquiez's finest hulls. Heavy displacement, predictable and reassuring motion in big seas — the comfort ratio ranks in the top tier of cruising yachts this size.

Only 21 ever built

The Amphitrite 45 MS was produced between 1986 and 1994. Only 21 hulls were completed. Finding one in this condition, ocean-proven, in Tahiti, ready to go — is genuinely rare.
